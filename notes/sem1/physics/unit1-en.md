# Unit 1: Wave Mechanics & Quantum Oscillations

Welcome to the premium core documentation for Wave Mechanics. This module outlines the mathematical framework governing wave propagation and state vectors.

## 1. Core Principles of Wave Mechanics

Wave motion is a disturbance that travels through a medium, transporting energy without permanently displacing the particles of the medium. 

> **Important Note:** In quantum mechanics, physical systems are represented by wave functions rather than definitive particle coordinates.

### Key Characteristics:
* **Amplitude ($A$):** The maximum displacement from equilibrium.
* **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** The distance between two consecutive crests.
* **Frequency ($f$):** Number of oscillations passing a point per second.

---

## 2. Mathematical Formulation

The classical one-dimensional wave equation is modeled using partial differential equations. The standard configuration is represented as:

$$\frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial x^2} = \frac{1}{v^2} \frac{\partial^2 \psi}{\partial t^2}$$

Where:
* $\psi$ represents the wave amplitude function.
* $v$ denotes the phase velocity of the propagating wave matrix.

---

## 3. Comparative Matrix: Classical vs Quantum Waves

| Property | Classical Waves | Quantum Waves (De Broglie) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Medium Requirement** | Elastic material medium required | No physical medium required |
| **Velocity Definition** | $v = f \times \lambda$ | Phase and Group velocities distinct |
| **Physical Reality** | Represents real particle displacement | Represents a probability amplitude ($\lvert\psi\rvert^2$) |
